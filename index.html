About Me

I am a Year 12 student at Herschel Grammar School, studying Maths, Further Maths, Computer Science, and Politics. My interest in Computer Science stems from the challenge of building and understanding complex systems, particularly through coding and game development. I primarily work with C# and Python, often using Unity to bring my ideas to life. While I occasionally use Blender for 3D modeling, I focus more on the programming side of game development.

I also have a solid foundation in web development, with experience in JavaScript, HTML, and CSS, which has helped me understand how to create functional, user-friendly designs. Whether I'm building a small game or experimenting with new coding techniques, I enjoy turning abstract concepts into working projects that challenge and inspire me.

While much of my work is independent, I value collaboration for the fresh perspectives it brings. Working with others has taught me how to approach problems creatively and how different ideas can shape a better final product.

Looking to the future, I'm excited to pursue a degree in Computer Science at university, where I aim to deepen my skills in software engineering and explore areas like artificial intelligence. I'm eager to take on more ambitious projects that push my programming abilities and expand my understanding of the field.
